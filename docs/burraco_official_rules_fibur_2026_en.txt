OFFICIAL ITALIAN BURRACO RULES REFERENCE (ONLINE SUMMARY)
Primary source: F.I.Bur. - Code of Play January 2026 Edition
PDF URL: https://www.burraconetwork.com/file/fibur/upload_notizie/QUMxb2p7NzNGPVQ4WlRBPFF1YWAI.pdf
Official news: https://www.fibur.it/News.aspx?Codice=9%7BbXf%2CE%5Em%3F%60%2AuXU%3F%28%7B%2B3
Source verification date: 2026-02-19

SCOPE
- F.I.Bur. rules for Italian Burraco (not the international variant).
- Text oriented to online validations/algorithms: physical table and referee workflows are excluded.

1) HAND BASE STRUCTURE
Ref.: Art. 1, 2, 4.
- 4 players in 2 partnerships.
- 2 decks of 54 cards (108 total, 4 jokers).
- Each player receives 11 cards.
- Two reserve piles (pozzetti) of 11 cards are prepared.
- Remaining cards form the draw pile; one face-up card starts the discard pile.

2) OBJECTIVE
Ref.: Art. 13, 17, 18.
- Hand: score more points than opponents with valid melds, burraco, and valid closing.
- Round/match: convert point difference (Match Points) into VP via official tables.

3) TURN FLOW
Ref.: Art. 7, 9.
1. Draw from draw pile or collect from discard pile.
2. Open new melds and/or lay off cards on existing melds.
3. Discard one card.
- Turn ends after discard.
- If the player collects from discard pile, that replaces drawing.
- If discard pile has only one card, that card cannot be discarded immediately unless the player has another identical card in hand.

4) OPENING/LAYING RULES (MELD VALIDATION)
Ref.: Definitions "Gioco"/"Legare", Art. 10, 11, 12.
- Open: create a valid meld with at least 3 cards.
- Lay off: add cards to your partnership line while keeping the meld always valid.
- Run: same suit, consecutive order, up to 13 cards + possible 1 wild.
- Set: same rank, from 3 to 8 cards + possible 1 wild.
- Wild: joker or pinella; maximum 1 wild per meld; exception: same-suit pinella used as natural 2 allows one additional wild in runs.
- Free wild in runs: lowest position; if an Ace is already low, highest position.
- Replaced joker/pinella stays in the same meld (does not return to hand).
- Sets made only of wild cards are not allowed.

5) BURRACO TYPES
Ref.: Art. 13.
- Burraco = run or set of at least 7 cards.
- Clean: no wilds used as wilds (pinella as natural 2 in runs still counts as clean).
- Semi-clean:
  run with 1 wild before or after at least 7 natural cards;
  set of 8 cards with 1 wild.
- Dirty: any burraco with wild usage outside clean/semi-clean cases.

6) HAND CLOSING
Ref.: Art. 14, 17, 34, 35.
- Valid close with +100: partnership has taken pozzetto, made at least one burraco, and closes by discarding.
- Close without +100 only in special cases: draw-pile exhaustion or timeout without a close.
- Closing by discarding a wild is not allowed.
- Closing without a discard is not allowed.
- Edge case: if the only available discard is a wild, close is invalid; last meld played is canceled and the wild remains as discard.

7) SCORING
Ref.: Art. 1, 13, 18.
Card values:
- Joker 30, Pinella 20, Ace 15, K/Q/J/10 10, 9-3 5.
Positive points:
- Clean burraco +200, semi-clean +150, dirty +100, closing +100.
- Plus the sum of cards in melds on table.
Negative points:
- Pozzetto not taken -100 (not applied if neither partnership takes pozzetto).
- Pozzetto taken but not played: negative sum of pozzetto cards.
- Cards left in hand: negative sum of card values.

8) WIN CONDITIONS
Ref.: Art. 18, 20.
- Hand win: higher total score.
- Round win: Match Points -> VP conversion via official table (0 points = 10-10 VP).
- Tournament win: final ranking by VP.
